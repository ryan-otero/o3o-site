TITLE: The "AI Guy at the Company" Playbook
TAG: idea
DATE: March 16, 2026
BODY: Most companies don't need an AI consultant — they need someone internal who already gets the business to start connecting dots. That's a different pitch than selling a retainer. The real move is becoming that person first, then formalizing it from the inside out.
The entry point is simple: find 2-3 real pain points, build small working solutions using no-code tools, and show results — not potential. One win gets you the room to do the next one. Document everything as you go. That becomes your portfolio.
Once you have a few wins, you start owning it formally. Ask for an "AI initiatives" function, even if it's informal. Track ROI. Time saved, cost reduced, revenue touched. That data is what makes the jump to external work credible.
By the time you go external, you're not starting from zero. You have deployed work, a repeatable process for diagnosing and solving AI problems, and deep knowledge of at least one industry. That's a stronger foundation than most consultants ever build.
