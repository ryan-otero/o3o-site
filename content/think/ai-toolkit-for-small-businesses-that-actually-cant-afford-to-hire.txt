TITLE: AI Toolkit for Small Businesses That Actually Can't Afford to Hire
TAG: idea
DATE: March 16, 2026
BODY: Most AI tools are built for people who already know how to use AI. Small businesses don't need that — they need something that does the job without a learning curve.
The idea: a stripped-down AI toolkit that onboards itself to your business first. You put in your background, location, customer type, tone — and everything it generates from that point is already calibrated to you. No prompting, no tweaking, no figuring it out.
The core offerings would cover the stuff small businesses are constantly doing manually: marketing materials (graphics, copy, captions, menus), hiring and recruiting content, invoices, and meeting notes — dump in a brain dump and it organizes it into something usable.
Competitor analysis is an interesting angle too. If it can pull publicly available info on local competitors, that's a feature a small business owner would actually pay for.
The onboarding layer is what makes it different. Without that, it's just another AI tool. With it, everything outputs feeling like it came from inside the business — not from a generic template.
